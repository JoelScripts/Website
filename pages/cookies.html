üìñ What Are Cookies?

üîç Definition

Cookies are small text files stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website. They are used to remember information about your visit, preferences, and browsing behavior.

Size: Typically a few kilobytes of data.

‚öôÔ∏è How Cookies Work

When you visit a website, the server sends a cookie to your browser, which stores it locally. On subsequent visits, your browser sends the cookie back to the website, allowing it to recognize you and remember your preferences or login information.

Process: Automatic client-server exchange.

‚è±Ô∏è Cookie Lifespan

Cookies can be either session-based (deleted when you close your browser) or persistent (stored for a specified duration). Their lifetime depends on the expiration date set by the website that creates them.

Duration: Minutes to years depending on type.

‚öîÔ∏è localStorage vs Cookies

üíæ What is localStorage?

localStorage is a browser feature that allows websites to store data locally on your device. Unlike cookies, localStorage data is not automatically sent to the server with each request. It persists until explicitly deleted by the user or website.

  • Larger storage capacity (5-10MB)
  • Only accessible to the website
  • Not sent with HTTP requests
  • Persists across browser sessions
Use: Client-side storage for preferences.

üîÑ Key Differences

Cookies and localStorage serve different purposes. Cookies are designed for client-server communication, while localStorage is for client-side storage. Understanding these differences helps you make informed choices about your privacy.

  • Cookies: Sent with every request
  • localStorage: Client-side only
  • Cookies: Smaller capacity
  • localStorage: Larger capacity
Privacy: Both should be considered.

üéØ Our Use of localStorage

We use localStorage only to store your timezone preference for improved user experience. This data is stored entirely on your device and is never sent to our servers or shared with third parties.

  • Timezone preference storage
  • No personal data collection
  • Entirely client-side
  • Deleted when you clear browser data
Control: Delete via browser settings anytime.

üóëÔ∏è How to Clear localStorage

Clearing localStorage is as simple as clearing your browser cookies. Open your browser developer tools (F12), go to the Application/Storage tab, and delete the stored data. You can also clear it when clearing your browsing data.

  • Open Developer Tools (F12)
  • Go to Application/Storage tab
  • Select localStorage
  • Click Delete or Clear All
Effect: Resets stored preferences.
